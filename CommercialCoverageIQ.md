# CoverageIQ — Commercial Viability Notes

*Started: March 19, 2026*

---

## Core Positioning

**Target user:** Writers — specifically writers preparing to submit to agents, managers, production companies, and networks.

**The pitch:**
> "Find out if your script will get a Pass before you send it to someone who matters."

**Framing:** Give writers insight into what the *gatekeepers* (agents, prodcos, networks) will think of their script — and specific, actionable feedback to improve it before it goes out.

---

## Why Writers Would Pay (Not Just Use ChatGPT)

1. **ChatGPT is too nice.** It's calibrated to encourage. A tool that gives genuine Pass verdicts — harshly, consistently — is more trustworthy precisely because it won't coddle. Writers know this.

2. **The format carries credibility.** Coverage is a specific industry artifact. A PDF with a verdict stamp, subscores, comps, and logline written in coverage voice *simulates* what the gatekeeper actually sees. That psychological reality is the product.

3. **Consistency and comparability.** Every script runs through the same rubric. Writers can resubmit revisions and track score improvement against a fixed standard. ChatGPT gives different output every time.

4. **Current buyer intelligence.** A knowledge base with what's selling now, what comps are resonating, what's in the room — not in ChatGPT's training data and hard to replicate with prompting.

5. **Friction removed.** Most writers don't know how to prompt for rigorous coverage. This is one click.

**The honest ceiling:** A sophisticated writer with good prompts can replicate ~70% of this from ChatGPT. But Grammarly, Turnitin, and a dozen other tools have built massive businesses in that same 30% gap — format, calibration, consistency, credibility. Most writers aren't that sophisticated, and the ones who are still value the format.

## Where We Distinguish Ourselves

**Nobody else has these:**

1. **Exec-calibrated verdict** — trained to think like a development exec evaluating acquisition. Real Pass verdicts when the script doesn't work. Not a writing teacher.

2. **"What will the gatekeeper think?" framing** — Every competitor helps writers improve. We simulate what the exec on the other side of the desk actually thinks. Different psychological hook entirely.

3. **Network/buyer fit scoring** — "This script scores 8/10 for Netflix, 4/10 for HBO, 6/10 for Hulu." No one does this. Writers would pay specifically for this.

4. **Inside industry knowledge** — Philip is a working development exec. That insider calibration — what's actually in the room, what comps are landing, what buyers are hungry for right now — is not replicable by any tool trained only on public data. This is the moat. It requires Philip's ongoing involvement to stay current, but it's real and defensible.

5. **Revision comparison** — submit a rewrite, see exactly what changed in the coverage scores, side by side. No one does this cleanly.

## ✅ MUST BUILD — Non-Negotiable (Philip confirmed March 19, 2026)

These four are definite. Not wishlist — committed.

1. **Script Chat / Q&A** — Let users ask follow-up questions about their script after coverage is generated. Prescene's killer feature. Nobody at our price point has it. This is a genuine differentiator.

2. **Free sample on first 10 pages** — Analyze the first 10 pages for free, full report requires payment. Proven conversion tool — ScriptReader built their user base on this model. Easy to implement, high impact.

3. **Scene-by-scene breakdown** — Not just an overall verdict. Specific notes on each scene: what's working, what isn't, what to cut. ScriptReader's most-cited strength. Writers need this to actually use the feedback.

4. **WGA compliance statement** — Explicit promise that submitted scripts are never stored beyond the analysis, never used for AI training. Cheap to implement, high trust value. Greenlight built credibility on this. Writers submitting original IP care deeply about it.
